Adoption

Our goal is to find a loving, life long home for each animal in our care. We strive to find the most suitable pet for each person and the most suitable home for each animal. To reach that goal, we consider both the lifestyle and expectations of potential adopters and the personality and needs of each animal. We welcome the opportunity to help match you with a wonderful companion and ask that you keep in mind the following guidelines to adopting from Acadiana Animal Aid. Please allow 3 days for our adoption coordinators to review your application and they will contact you.

Foster

A foster home is a temporary living situation for our shelter pets. Foster families provide shelter, care, and love while our pets await placement in a forever home or travel on a transport to an out-of-state shelter partner. The number of animals we can save depends entirely on the number of people who open their hearts and homes to foster pets. Foster homes allow us to create space in the shelter so we can rescue more pets in need.

Why Foster

Louisiana has a serious pet over population problem. Every day puppies and kittens enter municipal shelters and may not make it out alive. Fostering provides a life-saving outcome for these pets. Foster homes offer an alternative to the shelter environment and provide the attention and comfort that only a home setting can provide â€” especially for young kittens and puppies, older animals, and frightened pets who are having a hard time in the shelter. Foster pets usually stay in their temporary home anywhere from a few days to two weeks.

Fostering has so many rewards: you are able to help a pet in need, enjoy the companionship of a pet without a lifetime commitment, try new companions for the resident pets in your home, and provide some extra TLC to kittens and puppies too young to be adopted.
